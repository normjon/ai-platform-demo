# Performance Review Process

**Policy Owner:** HR Department
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2025
**Review Date:** 31 December 2025
**Classification:** INTERNAL

---

## Overview

The company operates an annual performance review cycle with a mid-year check-in. Reviews are designed to be a two-way conversation between employees and their line managers — focusing on achievements, development, and future goals.

---

## Annual Review Cycle

| Period | Activity |
|---|---|
| January–February | Goal-setting for the new year |
| July | Mid-year check-in (informal, no rating) |
| November–December | Annual review and rating |

---

## Annual Review Process

1. **Self-assessment:** Employee completes a self-assessment form in the HR system at least 1 week before the review meeting.
2. **Manager review:** Manager completes their assessment and ratings.
3. **Review meeting:** A dedicated 1-hour conversation covering achievements, development areas, and goals for the year ahead.
4. **Calibration:** Ratings are calibrated by the HR Business Partner across the team to ensure consistency.
5. **Final rating:** Communicated to the employee by end of January following the review period.

---

## Performance Ratings

| Rating | Description |
|---|---|
| Outstanding | Consistently exceeds all expectations; exceptional impact |
| Exceeds Expectations | Regularly exceeds most objectives and demonstrates strong performance |
| Meets Expectations | Fully meets all objectives; solid, reliable performance |
| Partially Meets Expectations | Met some but not all objectives; improvement required |
| Does Not Meet Expectations | Significantly below the required standard |

---

## Underperformance

Employees rated "Partially Meets" or "Does Not Meet Expectations" will be placed on a **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** developed in collaboration with HR. The PIP sets out clear, measurable objectives over a defined timeframe (typically 60–90 days) with regular review meetings.

---

## Pay Reviews

Performance ratings directly inform the annual pay review process. Pay increases are discretionary and determined by performance rating, market benchmarking, and company performance.
