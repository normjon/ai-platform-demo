# Annual Leave Policy

**Policy Owner:** HR Department
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2025
**Review Date:** 31 December 2025
**Classification:** INTERNAL

---

## Entitlement

Full-time employees are entitled to **25 days of paid annual leave** per calendar year, in addition to UK public holidays (typically 8 days). This gives a total of 33 days paid time off per year.

Part-time employees receive annual leave on a pro-rata basis calculated against the 25-day full-time entitlement. For example, an employee working 3 days per week receives 15 days of annual leave per year.

Employees on fixed-term contracts of less than one year accrue leave at the rate of 1/12th of the full entitlement per completed month of service.

New starters joining mid-year receive a pro-rata entitlement based on the number of full months remaining in the leave year.

---

## Leave Year

The leave year runs from **1 January to 31 December** each calendar year.

---

## Carry-Over

Up to **5 days of unused annual leave** may be carried forward into the following leave year. Carried-over leave must be taken by **31 March** of the following year or it is forfeited.

Employees who are unable to take leave due to long-term sickness or statutory leave (e.g., maternity, paternity, shared parental leave) may carry over additional leave in line with statutory requirements.

---

## Booking Leave

Employees must request annual leave via the HR self-service portal at least 2 weeks in advance for periods of up to 5 consecutive days, and at least 4 weeks in advance for periods of more than 5 consecutive days.

Managers are responsible for approving leave requests and must ensure adequate business cover is maintained. Requests may be declined during peak business periods.

---

## Public Holidays

UK public and bank holidays are in addition to the 25-day entitlement. Employees required to work on public holidays will receive a day off in lieu to be taken at a mutually agreed time.

---

## Sickness During Annual Leave

If an employee falls sick during a period of booked annual leave and obtains a Fit Note (formerly sick note) from their GP, the period of certified sickness may be recredited as annual leave and the days rescheduled.
