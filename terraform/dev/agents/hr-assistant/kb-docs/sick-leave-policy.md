# Sick Leave Policy

**Policy Owner:** HR Department
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2025
**Review Date:** 31 December 2025
**Classification:** INTERNAL

---

## Overview

The company is committed to supporting employees through periods of ill health. This policy sets out the entitlements and procedures for sick leave and pay.

---

## Sick Pay Entitlement

Employees are entitled to company sick pay as follows:

| Length of Service | Entitlement |
|---|---|
| 0–6 months | Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) only |
| 6 months–2 years | 4 weeks full pay, then SSP |
| 2–5 years | 8 weeks full pay, 4 weeks half pay, then SSP |
| 5+ years | 13 weeks full pay, 9 weeks half pay, then SSP |

Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) is paid at the current statutory rate (£116.75 per week as of 2025) for up to 28 weeks.

---

## Self-Certification

For absences of up to **7 calendar days** (including weekends), employees must complete a self-certification form via the HR portal on their first day back at work.

---

## Fit Note Requirement

Absences of **8 or more consecutive calendar days** require a Fit Note (medical certificate) from a GP or other qualified medical practitioner. The Fit Note must be submitted to the employee's line manager and HR.

---

## Reporting Procedure

1. Employees must notify their line manager **by 9:00 am** on the first day of absence, or as soon as reasonably practicable.
2. Notification should be by telephone where possible — voicemail or email alone is not sufficient.
3. Employees must provide regular updates to their line manager if the absence is expected to continue.

---

## Return to Work

Following any absence, the line manager will conduct a return-to-work discussion with the employee. This is a supportive conversation designed to welcome the employee back and identify any adjustments required.

---

## Employee Assistance Programme

Employees experiencing stress, anxiety, or other mental health conditions are encouraged to contact the **Employee Assistance Programme (EAP)** on **1800-EAP-HELP** (free, confidential, 24/7). The EAP provides counselling, financial advice, and legal guidance.
